Biographies page

Davy Jones - lead singer and drums

Davy Jones came from a background in acting staring at age 11 in Coronation Street - a popular British TV show.

He then trained as a jockey and later went on to play parts in some theatrical musicals.

In 1966 he was selected to be part of a television band known as the Monkees.

The band were soon in demand around the world. Jones had boyish good looks and a great sense of humour.

Soon he had many fans around the world, perfoming as their lead singer and playing the drums.

Mickey Dolenz - Drummer and lead vocals

Mickey Dolenz was a child actor. He was successful in his audition for the Monkees TV series in 1965

He wrote some of the bands songs including I'm a believer and Last train to Clarkesville.

He was a practical joker and good at getting humour going in the studio.

Mickey Dolenz is one of the three remaining members of the Monkees after Davy Jones died in 2013.

Dolenx has also appeared in many screen and stage shows in the UK and USA.

Michael Nesmith - vocals and guitarist

Michael Nesmith enjoyed writing poetry as a child. He enrolled in the US Air Force in 1960.

He wrote some of the bands songs and performed these before he was in the band winning an award for folksongs.

He rode a motorbike to the Monkees audtion and wore a woolly hat to keep the wind out of his eyes!.

Michael Nesmith is one of the three remaining members of the Monkees after Davy Jones died in 2013.

Nesmith has also appeared in many screen and stage shows in the UK and USA.

Peter Tork - Keyboardist and Bass Guitarist

Peter Tork started playing musical instruments aged 9. Did the introduction for Daydream believer in 1967

He was very close to his grandmother with grandma managing his fan club for many years.

He did some solo recordings in the 1980s.

Peter Tork is one of the three remaining members of the Monkees after Davy Jones died in 2013.

Tork has also appeared in many screen and stage shows in the UK and USA.
